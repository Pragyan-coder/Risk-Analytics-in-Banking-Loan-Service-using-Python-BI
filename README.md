# 📈Risk Analysis & Customer Segmentation

### ✅Project Details:

- Performed Data Manipulation & EDA using Python (Pandas) and developed a dashboard with Power BI to generate important insights 
- Client: York City Bank, Vermont (US)
- Domain: Banking & Finance (Loan) 
- Tools used: Excel, Jupyter Notebook, Power BI
  
This project demonstrates an end-to-end **Data Analysis** Process using various tools – from Excel/CSV to Power BI and analytics techniques used for exploring, cleaning, and analyzing a Customer Loan dataset.

### ✅Problem statement:

- Analyze a ‘$4.3 Bn Customer Loan’ dataset for **York City Bank** to help them minimize the risk of losing money.
  
YCB, a leading banking conglomerate based in Vermont, seeks deeper insight into customer profiles to strengthen underwriting and improve loan-approval decisions. Management has observed concerning patterns and anomalies in money lending across demographics and wants to uncover actionable insights into applicant's financial stability—income consistency, savings adequacy, and overall creditworthiness. Key questions include whether an applicant has a stable income and sufficient savings, whether they should be approved for a loan, and whether they are likely to repay it.

- As an Analyst, I am tasked with analyzing the company’s consumer profile & their financial status to answer those overarching business questions

### ✅The Impact: Measurable results

-	Europe accounts for the largest share of the loan portfolio ($765 M)
-	Age group 18–29 seems to be the medium risk segment with a 43.51 Risk score
-	The 60+ age group represents the highest loan exposure at $1,613.9M, as well as business lending at $959 M, suggesting that YCB prioritizes high-volume lending to the high age group
-	"Very High" income customers carry an average risk score of 56.55, despite maintaining the lowest DTI ratio at 10.0%.
-	High Income Bands follow closely with an average risk score of 55.88, while Medium Income Band represents an average score of 46.65.
-	Low Income Band has the lowest average risk score at 27.86 (High Risk Category)
-	"Executive Secretary" is the top occupation by risk score, allowing for more stringent credit vetting in these areas.
-	Property (Score 20) accounts for $878.6M (50.4%) loans, while Property (Score -10) has a loan volume of $391.4M (22.5%)
- Property (Score 20) represents the largest portion of the portfolio

By synthesizing demographic data with financial performance metrics, this project transforms a Loan portfolio into a segmented, manageable roadmap for York City Bank. The findings suggest that while the bank is heavily exposed to high-risk clients (approx. 43.3%), the true volatility lies within younger age groups (20.33%) with an average risk score and high-income/low-DTI segments. This analysis provides the bank with the necessary intelligence to adjust its lending criteria and move toward a more balanced, moderate-risk portfolio.
